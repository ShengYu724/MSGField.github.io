MSGField: A Unified Scene Representation Integrating Motion, Semantics, and Geometry for Robotic Manipulation

Yu Sheng , Runfeng Lin , Lidian Wang , Quecheng Qiu , Yanyong Zhang , Yu Zhang , Bei Hua , Jianmin Ji
University of Science and Technology of China Institute of Artificial Intelligence, Hefei Comprehensive National Sci- ence Center, Hefei, Anhui, China
Code arXiv

Abstract

An accurate, comprehensive, and efficient scene representation is crucial for robotic manipulation. Existing methods for dynamic scenes either fail to update in real-time or rely on additional pose estimation for simple edits, making it challenging to capture complex motion. To address these challenges, we introduce MSGField, a representation that uses a collection of 2D Gaussians for high-quality reconstruction, further enhanced with attributes encoding both semantic and motion information. We optimize the motion of each primitive in space, decomposing it into a combination of a limited number of motion bases. This approach captures complex motion while maintaining representation compactness. Additionally, we designed a pipeline that leverages object priors to quickly obtain object-centric semantics. Thanks to the compact nature of our representation, we are able to generate semantic fields and capture object motion within seconds. In our challenging dataset, which includes flexible and small objects, our method achieved a success rate of 78.4% in static environments and 63.3% in dynamic environments. The code and dataset will be available at: https://git.ustc.edu.cn/shengyu/msgfiled.git
